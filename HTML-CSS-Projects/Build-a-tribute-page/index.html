Nikola Tesla

A genius far ahead of his time with a lot of misfortunes

Here's a time line of Nikola Tesla:

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla was born
  • 1870 - Tesla attends "Higher Real Gynmasium" and graduated a year early.
  • 1881 - Tesla worked for an Budapest Telephone Exchange in hungary. During his employment, he made numerous improvements to the company to make them operational.
  • 1884 - Nikola Tesla would arrive in America.
  • 1887 - Tesla was introduced to Alfred S. Brown, director of Western Union, and Charles F. Peck, a New York City attorney. This was the turning point for Tesla where he recieved funding on his alternating current system.
  • 1890 - Nikola Tesla discovered wireless power after illuminating a vacuum tube wirelessly which begin his obssession of wireless transmission of energy.
  • 1891 - A contract signed with Westinghouse allowed for the install of Tesla A.C. power system, which transmit alternating currents at high voltage for power purposes, for a long distance.
  • 1898 - Tesla demonstrated his "automation" technology by wireless controlling a model boat.
  • 1899 - Tesla claimed discovery of a new geo-electrical phenomenon, which should allow for the transport of electricity around the world.
  • 1901 - Construction finally begins for Tesla's most ambitious project, The World-Wide Wireless System known as Wardenclyffe Tower.
  • 1904 - Unfortunately, the funding for his project to create electricity transformatioin around the world, which would be the beginning of Tesla's misfortunes.
  • 1916 - Although Tesla had minor sucesses, he fell deeper and deeper into debt. Tesla was in a financial crisis.
  • 1943 - Tesla passed away quietly and alone, at the age of 86, in his room at Hotel New Yorker in New York City.
